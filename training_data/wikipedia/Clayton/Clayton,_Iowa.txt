Clayton is a city in Clayton County, Iowa, United States.
The population was 43 at the 2010 census, down from 55 at the 2000 census.
Clayton is located directly on the Mississippi River and is only accessible by a very steep road.
Many people have summer homes in Clayton due to its proximity to the Mississippi River.
Clayton was founded in 1849.
Clayton is located at 42°54′11″N 91°8′53″W (42.902929, -91.148165).
According to the United States Census Bureau, the city has a total area of 0.49 square miles (1.27 km2), all of it land.

50.0% of all households were made up of individuals and 14.2% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older.
The average household size was 1.54 and the average family size was 2.07.
The median age in the city was 59.3 years.
2.3% of residents were under the age of 18; 0% were between the ages of 18 and 24; 13.9% were from 25 to 44; 51.2% were from 45 to 64; and 32.6% were 65 years of age or older.
The gender makeup of the city was 46.5% male and 53.5% female.
